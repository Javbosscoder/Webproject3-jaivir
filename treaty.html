Location of Negotiation

Treaty 1 was negotiated at Fort Garry (modern-day Winnipeg, Manitoba) in 1871. This location was significant as it was a major trading post and served as a key settlement hub for European settlers at the time.

Map of Treaty Area

Treaty 1 covers lands in Manitoba, extending into parts of Saskatchewan and Alberta. A map would show the specific territory of the Treaty, along with a larger map highlighting where this area lies within Canada.

Communities Involved

The Treaty involved the Cree, Saulteaux, and Ojibwe nations. These communities, primarily in Manitoba, continue to be part of Treaty 1, with descendants living in modern First Nations like Peguis, Brokenhead, and Long Plain.

Historic Terms

Treaty 1 promised land, annuities, provisions, and rights to hunt and fish. It allocated 160 acres per family of five, offered annual payments, and provided agricultural tools, although many promises were underdelivered, leading to long-term grievances.

What Makes Treaty 1 Unique

As the first of the Numbered Treaties, Treaty 1 set the stage for future negotiations but had vague terms and unmet promises. The Treaty highlighted the growing tension between Indigenous rights and settler expansion in the region.

Implementation Today

Treaty 1 continues to be implemented through urban reserves, like the Kapyong Urban Reserve, and economic development projects in First Nations communities. Modern efforts also focus on self-governance, land entitlement negotiations, and cultural revitalization programs.
